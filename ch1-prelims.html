Company Rule (1773–1858)

1773 Regulating Act of 1773

🧠 Memory Aid

FRSC — First step, Recognised political functions, Supreme Court (Calcutta 1774), Central administration foundation

Warren Hastings = First GGoB. Hastings was in haste to govern Bengal first.

1781 Amending Act of 1781 Act of Settlement

🧠 Memory Aid

REAP — Revenue excluded, Exempted GG, Appeals to GG-in-Council, Personal law applied

The Act that clipped the Supreme Court's wings.

1784 Pitt's India Act of 1784 East India Act 1784

🧠 Memory Aid

Double Pitt — Pitt's Act = Double Government (Board of Control + Court of Directors)

BoC = Political. CoD = Commercial. CoD and BoC both abolished by GoI Act 1858.

1786 Act of 1786

🧠 Memory Aid

Cornwallis wanted 2 things — Override Council + Commander-in-Chief. Corn needs two cobs.

1793 Charter Act of 1793

🧠 Memory Aid

1793 = Extended everything — Extended overriding powers, extended trade monopoly (20 years), extended GG's control over presidencies.

1813 Charter Act of 1813

🧠 Memory Aid

1813 = Opened India — Opened trade, missionaries, western education. Called "Foundation Stone of English Education in India."

Exception: Tea and China trade monopoly continued. Tea is too precious to share yet.

1833 Charter Act of 1833 Saint Helena Act

🧠 Memory Aid

1833 = Bengal to India — GGoB became GGoI. Bentick was first. Bentick bent the title from Bengal to India.

Regulations → Acts — Before 1833 = Regulations. After 1833 = Acts.

Bonus: Abolished slavery 40 years before the US. Seed for Article 15 (no discrimination).

1853 Charter Act of 1853 Final Charter Act

🧠 Memory Aid

1853 = Three Firsts — First separation of legislative/executive, First open competition for civil services, First local representation in Central Legislature.

Macaulay 1854 — Committee came one year after the Act. Mac came after the Act.

Cornwallis = Father of Indian Civil Service (not Macaulay). Macaulay introduced reforms.

Crown Rule (1858–1947)

1858 Government of India Act of 1858 Act for Good Governance of India

🧠 Memory Aid

1858 = Crown takes over — Company abolished, GGoI becomes Viceroy, Double Govt ended, Secretary of State created.

Canning = First Viceroy. Canning canned the Company.

15-member Council of India — abolished by GoI Act 1935.

1861 Indian Councils Act of 1861

🧠 Memory Aid

3 Indians in 1862 — BPD: Benaras, Patiala, Dinkar Rao. BPD = British-Period Delegates.

Ordinance = 6 months — Still 6 months today under Article 123.

1863: Satyendranath Tagore — First Indian ICS officer.

1892 Indian Councils Act of 1892

🧠 Memory Aid

1892 = Almost elections — Introduced indirect elections but never used the word 'election'. Called it "nomination on recommendation."

1892 was too shy to say election.

1909 Indian Councils Act of 1909 Morley-Minto Reforms

🧠 Memory Aid

Morley-Minto = More + Communal — More members (16→60), More Indians (Sinha first), More division (communal electorates).

Sinha = First Indian in Viceroy's Executive Council. Sinha shone first.

Minto = Father of Communal Electorate. Minto minced the electorate into communities.

1919 Government of India Act of 1919 Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms

🧠 Memory Aid

Montagu-Chelmsford = DEBT — Dyarchy, Elections (direct, first time), Bicameralism (first time), Transferred/Reserved subjects

Dyarchy — Transferred = Ministers responsible to legislature. Reserved = Governor NOT responsible. Reserved = Restricted democracy.

Council of State = Upper House. State is above Assembly.

Simon Commission 1927 — All 7 British → All parties boycotted → "Simon Go Back"

1927 Simon Commission Indian Statutory Commission

🧠 Memory Aid

Simon = All British, All Boycotted — 7 members, all British, chaired by Sir John Simon. No Indian members → nationwide boycott → "Simon Go Back."

Formed 2 years early (1927 instead of 1929). Report submitted 1930. Led to 3 Round Table Conferences → White Paper → Joint Committee → GoI Act 1935.

1932 Communal Award Ramsay MacDonald Award

🧠 Memory Aid

Communal Award → Poona Pact — MacDonald extended separate electorates to Depressed Classes → Gandhi fasted unto death at Yeravada Jail → Poona Pact between Gandhi and Ambedkar → No separate electorate but reserved seats in Hindu joint electorate.

MacDonald divided, Gandhi united through Poona Pact.

1935 Government of India Act of 1935

🧠 Memory Aid

321 Sections, 10 Schedules — 3-2-1 countdown to Independence.

Three lists — Federal 59, Provincial 54, Concurrent 36. F-P-C = 59-54-36. Descending order.

6 bicameral provinces — BB MABU: Bengal, Bombay, Madras, Assam, Bihar, United Provinces

RBI + Federal Court both established by 1935 Act. Both set up in 1937.

Note: Mindmap says 10% population franchise — Laxmikanth says 14%. Use 14% for exam.

1947 Indian Independence Act of 1947

🧠 Memory Aid

Key dates — Feb 20 (Attlee announces) → June 3 (Mountbatten Plan) → Aug 15 (Independence)

Mountbatten = Last Viceroy + First Governor-General of India. He stayed to see it through.

Pakistan included — West Punjab, Sindh, Baluchistan, East Bengal, NWFP, Sylhet (Assam)

GoI Act 1935 governed India till Jan 26, 1950 when Constitution came into force.

1946 Interim Government of India (1946)

🧠 Memory Aid

Nehru = Vice-President of Council — Viceroy remained head. Members were part of Viceroy's Executive Council.

Members who chose Pakistan — I.I. Chundrigar, Liaquat Ali Khan, Asaf Ali, Abdur Rab Nishtar, Ghaznafar Ali Khan.

Remember: Ambedkar was NOT in Interim Government — he joined only after Independence in the first cabinet.

1947 First Cabinet of Independent India (1947)

🧠 Memory Aid

Nehru = PM. Patel = States + Retained portfolios. Ambedkar joined here as Law Minister — not in Interim Government.

Shanmugham Chetty = First Finance Minister of independent India.

Rajkumari Amrit Kaur = First woman cabinet minister (Health).
